Tech Cornwall Stock Take App Project Overview  
Welcome to the Project  
This project is an opportunity to build a real application that will be used by a real 
organisation.  
Rather than creating software purely for assessment purposes, your work will solve a 
genuine problem faced by Tech Cornwall. At the end of the project, the aim is to have a 
working application that staff can use on their own mobile phones to complete an an nual 
stock take.  
Throughout the project you should remember that your goal is not simply to write code. 
Your goal is to make a task that currently requires time, concentration and manual 
recording as quick, simple and reliable as possible.  
This document explains the story behind the application, why it is needed, and how it will 
be used. The accompanying project documents contain the detailed requirements and 
should always be treated as the source of truth when making design or implementatio n 
decisions.  
The documents provided alongside this overview are:  
• Developer Handoff Pack . This explains the functional requirements, business 
rules and expected behaviour of the application.  
• Catalogue Behaviour Specification . This describes how users interact with the 
catalogue and the principles behind the user interface.  
• Stock Take Mobile Web App, Agile User Stories and Acceptance Criteria . 
This contains the complete list of user stories and acceptance criteria. These 
stories should be completed in the order they appear, as each section builds 
naturally on the previous one.  
Whenever there is uncertainty, these documents should take priority over this overview.  
 
    
 
The Story  
Tech Cornwall owns a variety of equipment that is used throughout the year.  
Some equipment is used within Tech Cornwall itself, while other items belong to Agile on 
the Beach. Equipment is stored across two different locations and may be moved 
between them throughout the year.  
At least once each year, every item needs to be counted. This information is used to 
understand what equipment is available and to support the organisation’s end of year 
asset valuation.  
Instead of carrying paper lists or trying to use a laptop in storage areas, staff should be 
able to complete the entire stock take using their own mobile phones.  
The application you will build makes this possible.  
The app allows staff to walk around each location, find equipment, record what they 
discover, note the condition of items and capture serial numbers where required. At any 
point during the process they can download the latest results as a single file, allo wing 
progress to be reviewed before the stock take is complete.  
The final result should be a simple, reliable application that produces consistent results 
regardless of which member of staff carries out the stock take.  
The Problem  
Imagine arriving at a storage room containing shelves of equipment.  
There may be monitors, laptops, networking equipment, cables, accessories, event 
equipment and many other items.  
Trying to remember everything before writing it down is slow.  
Using a desktop computer is impractical because the equipment is spread around the 
room.  
Using paper creates additional work because someone must later transfer everything 
into a spreadsheet.  
The purpose of this application is to remove those problems.  
Instead of writing notes, the user simply opens the application on their phone, selects 
where they are working and taps through the catalogue as they discover equipment.  
The application becomes a digital clipboard that guides the user through the counting 
process while keeping the information organised automatically.
